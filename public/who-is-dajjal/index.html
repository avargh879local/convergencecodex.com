Who Is Al-Masih ad-Dajjal?

Dajjal is Islam's great end-time deceiver. In English he is often called the Islamic Antichrist, but that shortcut can confuse the comparison because Dajjal and the Biblical Antichrist do not map perfectly.

Short Answer

Al-Masih ad-Dajjal is a deceptive messianic figure who appears before the final judgment, performs signs, misleads many people, and is ultimately killed by Isa ibn Maryam in Islamic tradition.

In many hadith reports, Dajjal is physically marked, associated with false miracles, and identified as the greatest trial before the Hour.

Why This Matters

If Dajjal is treated as the exact Islamic equivalent of the Antichrist, the Mahdi-Antichrist thesis weakens. But if Dajjal is compared with the False Prophet or with a rival deceiver role, the broader mirror-image theory remains open.

This is why the site separates vocabulary from structure. "Antichrist" is Biblical and Christian terminology. "Dajjal" is Islamic terminology. The work is to compare roles without pretending the traditions say the same thing directly.
