About

The Convergence Codex

The Convergence Codex is a living comparative eschatology project. It studies how Islamic and Biblical traditions describe final rulers, deceivers, messianic figures, Gog and Magog, and the last conflict before judgment.

Purpose

The purpose is research, not hostility. The site asks hard questions about Al-Mahdi, Dajjal, the Antichrist, the False Prophet, Isa, and the returning Christ while keeping the traditions distinct and the evidence visible.

The project is written for beginners, Bible students, Muslim readers, Christian readers, and secular researchers who want a structured map rather than scattered debate clips.

Editorial Commitments

Primary Sources FirstBible, Quran, and hadith references come before modern debate claims.
Label The LensChristian, Islamic, and secular claims are not blended together as if they use the same assumptions.
Admit WeaknessesIf a hadith is disputed or a comparison is speculative, the site should say so plainly.
No Religious HateThe site critiques interpretations and arguments, not the dignity of religious communities.
